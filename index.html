About Me

My name is Alexander (Alex) Lalejini, and I am a fourth year computer science student at Mississippi State University. I have a 4.0 GPA and plan to graduate in the Spring of 2015. I am particularly interested in robotics and artificial intelligence; more specifically, I am interested in the application of biomimetics as applied to robotics and artificial intelligence. My interests have led me to become involved in the Social, Therapeutic, and Robotics Systems (STaRS) Lab at Mississippi State University where I work as an undergraduate research assistant. Through the Naval Research Enterprise Internship Program, I worked in the Laboratory for Autonomous Systems Research (LASR) at the Naval Research Laboratory in Washington D.C. during the summer of 2014. In the past, I have interned at the Naval Research Laboratory at John C. Stennis Space Center, Mississippi and the Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, California; I have also worked as an undergraduate research assistant in the physics department at Mississippi State University.
